documents and content. You can apply filtering, save searches, customize search results, and
export results.

  default.

## Search requirements

- A keyword search must contain at least four characters to execute.

## OCR and Text Extraction Create Searchable Content for Uploaded Documents in Docusign CLM

When you upload a document, Docusign CLM uses Optical Character Recognition (OCR) or text
extraction to make your content searchable.

OCR uses technology to identify characters in scanned documents. When you upload a
document as an image file, it is automatically processed using OCR. Results of text
search depend on the quality of the image uploaded. Handwritten text on images is
not processed using OCR.

OCR is used when the following document types are uploaded to CLM:

- .JPG
- .JPEG
- .PNG
- .TIF
- .TIFF

Note: PDF files with less than 150 text
characters, including spaces, are processed with OCR. If your PDF contains 150 text
characters or more, text extraction is used. See the text extraction section below
for more information. If you upload a scanned PDF and your scanner uses OCR, the
text will already be extracted from the PDF and will not be processed using OCR in
CLM.

Text extraction scans and extracts text from PDF and DOCX documents. When you upload
a PDF or DOCX with 150 characters or more, they are automatically processed using
text extraction. Text is not extracted from documents with more than 500 pages. If
you have a document over 500 pages, you can split the document into multiple files
to ensure your text is extracted and searchable

## Search functionality

- Search in Docusign CLM searches by the prefix of words, not postfix or the
  middle of words.
- By default, a search only queries the most recent version of documents. To query
  document versions, use the Search all document versions filter in advanced
  search.
- The search results page displays up to 100 results by default. Administrators
  can configure the maximum number of search results to display (100 to 1000) in Admin > Account Management > Account Settings > Search Results Display.
- Search results only display content the user performing the search has access
  to.
- Use quotation marks to execute a phrase search.
- Stop words are commonly used words such as articles, pronouns, and prepositions.
  Docusign CLM ignores the following stop words both when indexing entries for
  search and when retrieving them as a result of a search: a, an, and, are, as,
  at, be, but, by, for, if, in, into, is, it, no, not, of, on, or, such, that,
  the, their, then, there, these, they, this, to, was, will, with.

## Search examples

- **App:** This search will not execute. A search must contain at
  least four characters to execute successfully.
- **Appl:** All words starting with the letters *a-p-p-l.*
  Example results: **appl**e, **Appl**eton, **appl**es.
- **Apple:** The word *apple* and all stemmed versions of the
  word. Example results: **apple**s, **apple** pie, **apple** banana
  pie.
- **Apple pies:** The words *apple* and *pies,* first
  looking for results with both words and then results with one of the words.
  Example results: **Apple pies, apple, pies**
- **“Apple pies”:** The phrase “apple pies”, and results with stop words such
  as “apple and pies”, but not “apple banana pies”. Example results: **apple pie,
  apple and pie, apple** or **pies.**
