# Docusign CLM Process Flow Diagram

Read about process flow diagrams in Docusign CLM and how users can use them for mapping
out an overview of important components and processes before CLM implementation.

All agreements that an organization creates and reviews go through a process in which it
is prepared, acted upon, signed, and managed. As a result, before implementing Docusign CLM, you must create a diagram to map out the process.

Your CLM team helps you refine your existing process flow diagram or create a new one
during the discovery phase. Your team also helps improve your current and future
processes.

If your process flow diagram is not well-defined before the discovery phase, it is best
to outline a framework by answering the following questions:

- Who are the actors involved in the document review process, and what are their
  roles?
- How do contracts enter the process flow, and how does the central actor
  respond?
- What actions does the system, meaning CLM, need to perform?
- Is there a strategy in place to deal with setbacks?
- Are there any special requirements for approval?

First, create your process flow diagram by defining the actors and their roles. Actors
can be clients or end-users, legal or finance departments, attorneys, or internal users.
The CLM system is also an actor because you must decide the actions it performs. For
example, is CLM routing documents conditionally or sending emails or notifications to
alert activity?

Next, consider how a newly prepared contract enters the process flow and how the central
actor responds. For example, central actors commonly route documents to different review
parties within an organization.

Then, plan exception paths for when rejections occur. For example, if a client refuses to
sign a document due to an error, an appropriate cancellation path is necessary to route
the document back to the actor responsible for making corrections.

Lastly, decide if there are any special requirements involved in the process. For
example, a $500k contract might need special approval from the SVP.
