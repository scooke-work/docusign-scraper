# Document Types in Docusign CLM

Learn more about each of the document types in Docusign CLM.

Docusign CLM has three broad categories for document types: standalone documents, amendments,
and supplements. Knowing these categories and the document types in each category
can help administrators determine alternate names and visibility.

## Standalone Documents

These are the primary documents in an agreement. They can be related to other
standalone documents, or be modified by agreements. By default, these document types
are classified as standalone documents:

- Master Service Agreement
- Memorandum of Understanding
- Terms and Conditions
- Letter of Intent
- Quote
- Purchase Order
- Proposal
- Order Form
- Statement of Work
- License
- Service Level Agreement
- Distribution
- Engagement Letter
- Fee
- Lease
- Retainer
- Nondisclosure Agreement
- Services Agreement
- Other

## Amendments

These documents serve to modify another document in an agreement within the same
party, and are not standalone documents. By default, these document types are
classified as amendments:

- Addendum
- Amendment
- Change Order

## Supplements

These documents give additional information or context to an agreement. Typically,
they are viewed and acknowledged, but not signed. By default, these document types
are classified as supplements:

- Appendix
- Exhibit
- Attachment
- Supplemental Document
