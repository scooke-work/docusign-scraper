# Playbook Rule Optimization in the AI-Assisted Review add-in for Agreement Desk

Learn about how to format legal review questions into playbook rules in the Docusign AI-Assisted Review add-in for Agreement Desk.

Sometimes, playbook rules are formatted differently for your team from how they would be
written as instructions for AI-Assisted Review. Here are some examples of common
changes to optimize your playbook rules.

| Before | After | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Is this NDA mutual or one-way? | NDA must be mutual. | Rules should be stated as instructions, not questions. Convert questions to statements and think about what case you want flagged for review to make your assertion. |
| Governing law must be Washington, Delaware, or Georgia. | Governing law must be Washington, Delaware, or Georgia. If none of these is listed, change the governing law to Delaware. | You should aim to cover the main cases that arise in your instructions. Otherwise, if “Utah” appears, the add-in doesn’t know which of your 3 allowable states you prefer to be listed. |
| Flag any term length longer than 3 years. | The term length must be less than 3 years. | The add-in isn’t programmed to flag, highlight, or perform other operations outside of providing you a valuable suggestion. Focus on the requirement itself in your rule and the flagging part will be handled. |
| No non-standard payment terms. | Payment terms must be NET 45 or fewer. | You need to clarify what you consider non-standard so the add-in can identify non-standard terms. |
| If the counterparty changes the term length to be less than one year, reject this change. | Term length must be 1 year or greater. | The add-in runs over the current state of the document and doesn’t see which party markup came from. The rule should be written to reflect the requirement itself, assuming the counterparty edit is applied. |

If you are not getting the results you want from rules you have made, here are some
tips for tuning your rules for better results.

| Case | Recommendation |
| --- | --- |
| Rule doesn’t match the correct section. | Sometimes this happens if there are very similar sections in a contract. Add more context in your description to help the add-in find the correct section. This could be alluding to the section name, the objective of the section, or any other details about your requirement. |
| Rule doesn’t give me an adequate suggestion. | Be specific about what you want to be stipulated in the add-in output. Think about additions to the rule that you would explain to someone who knows nothing about your business. You should guide the add-in to the outcomes you expect by being as specific as needed. |
| Rule doesn’t match all of the sections I expect in my document. | Provide more details in your rule to ensure the AI can identify all relevant sections. |
