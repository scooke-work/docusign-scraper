# English (U.S.) Language Support

Read about the Agreement Manager AI models and features that are supported in English agreements for customers located in the United States (U.S.).

Agreement Manager is available to customers globally, however, the features and insights vary by region and language. Read about the supported agreement types and attributes that provide insights into English agreements for customers in the United States (U.S.).

CAUTION: The accuracy and appropriateness of AI-assisted insights may vary depending on the agreement language and customer use case. Customers should review all suggested data points for accuracy and to determine if a value is appropriate for its particular circumstances.

See the [Language and Regional Support](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=xvp1729795957126.html&_LANG=enus "Read about language support and regional availability for Agreement Manager AI models and features.") article to view supported agreement types, attributes, and data points for other languages and regions.

## Supported attributes (fields)

During agreement processing, attributes and field values are surfaced in your agreements. Review the table to see which attributes are surfaced in English (U.S.) agreements.

Important: English (global) is applied to all customers deployed across Docusign datacenters located in the United States (NA), Europe (EU), Canada (CA), Australia (AU), and Japan (JP).

| Attribute | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Assignment and Change of Control Insights** | |
| Assignment (Change of Control) | Indicates whether the agreement allows for the assignment of rights and obligations to another party. For example, in case of a merger and acquisition (M&A) or other change of control activity. |
| Assignment (General) | Indicates whether the agreement allows one of the parties to assign its rights and obligations to another party. |
| Assignment (Termination Rights) | Indicates whether the agreement allows for termination if one of the parties assigns its rights and obligations to another party. |
| **Compensation and Payment Insights** | |
| Annual Contract Value | The average yearly revenue generated from a contract or customer subscription. It is commonly used in subscription-based businesses to measure the annualized value of a contract, helping organizations track recurring revenue over time. |
| Total Contract Value | The total monetary value of the contract or agreement between two or more parties. In other words, it is the lifetime value of a contract. |
| Late Fees Apply | Indicates whether the agreement explicitly states that if payment is late fees, charges, or interest will be added. |
| Late Fee % | The percentage of the contract value that is charged when late fees are incurred. |
| Payment Terms | The conditions set by a seller regarding when and how payment for goods or services is expected from the buyer. These terms typically specify the time frame for payment, for example, *Net 30* means payment is due 30 days after the invoice date. |
| Price Cap Increase % | The maximum allowable price increase under the agreement. |
| **Confidentiality Insights** | |
| Duration of Confidentiality | Indicates the length of time that the obligations of an NDA remain in effect. |
| **Governing Law, Jurisdiction, and Dispute Resolution Insights** | |
| Governing Law | The rules and laws that will govern the agreement if legal issues or disputes arise. The clause typically identifies whose laws will govern the interpretation and enforcement of the agreement. |
| Jurisdiction | The legal jurisdiction of an agreement in case of a future dispute. This value is typically listed as a specific county or city. |
| **Limitation of Liability Insights** | |
| Liability Cap Amount | The maximum amount or limit that a party will pay for damages or any liability in a specific dollar amount. |
| Liability Cap Duration | Indicates the length of time during which claims can be made under the liability cap within the agreement. |
| Liability Cap Multiplier | The limit a party will pay for damages or any liability, including a multiplier that can increase the cap amount under certain conditions. |
| **Objective Information Insights** | |
| Execution Date | The official date that the agreement was signed by all parties. |
| Languages | Indicates which languages are present in the agreement. |
| NDA Type | Indicates the type of NDA that is in place for the agreement. For example, mutual or one-way. |
| Parties | Contracting parties. Refers to a provision naming the individuals, organizations, or entities that enter into a legally binding agreement (contract). |
| Title | The official title of the agreement. |
| Type (Agreement Type) | Classification of documents based on their title and certain commonalities in their subject matter. For example, Master Services Agreement (MSA), Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDA), and Statement of Work (SOW). |
| **Payment Obligation Insights** | |
| Deposit or Initial Payment Amount | The first sum of money that is paid upfront when entering into an agreement. |
| Deposit or Initial Payment Amount - Calculated | The calculated amount paid upfront when entering into an agreement. This value uses text to calculate the amount if not explicitly written in the agreement. |
| Deposit or Initial Payment Amount - Due Date | The date the deposit or initial payment is due. |
| Deposit or Initial Payment Amount - Period Due | The duration of time within which a deposit or initial payment is due. |
| Late Payment Penalties | The penalty for late payments. |
| Late Payment Interest | The interest rate levied on late payments. |
| One Time Payment Amount | A single payment made on a single occasion. The payment is only made once for a specific amount. The payment is not part of a recurring payment schedule. |
| One Time Payment - Due Date | The specific date by which a single, non-recurring payment must be made in full to avoid late fees or penalties. |
| One Time Payment - Period Due | The duration of time within which a one-time payment is due. |
| Early Payment Discounts | A financial incentive for making a payment before the due date. |
| Promisor | The party that is responsible for fulfilling an obligation. |
| **Renewal Insights** | |
| Automatic Renewal (Renewal Type) | Renewal type. Refers to provisions stating that an agreement automatically renews at the end of its term unless one or both parties take action to terminate, opt out, or modify it. |
| Evergreen (Renewal Type) | Renewal type. Refers to clauses that contemplate a perpetual continuation of the contract term unless a party takes action to terminate it. |
| Option to Renew (Renewal Type) | Renewal type. Refers to provisions that set out a party's option to extend the term of the contract. |
| NonRenewal Notice Period | Refers to the period of time required for a party to give notice of their intent not to renew an agreement. |
| Renewal - Extension Period | Refers to provisions setting out the additional period of time (duration) that a contract is to be extended beyond its initial term. |
| Renewal Notice Period | Comprised of two data extractions: Renewal Notice Period and NonRenewal Notice Period. Refers to the period of time required for a party to give notice of their intent to renew an agreement. |
| Term - Renewal Automatic | Refers to provisions that specify the duration of the auto-renewal period. |
| Term - Renewal Option | Refers to provisions setting out a party's option to extend the contract for a specific term. |
| **Term Insights** | |
| Effective Date | Refers to the date on which the contract became legally binding and enforceable. It is the date agreed upon by the parties for beginning the period of performance under the contract. |
| Expiration Date | Refers to the date on which the contract comes to an end and is no longer in force. |
| Term Length | Indicates the length of the term of the agreement (how long the agreement is in effect). Signing parties are obligated to adhere to the terms and conditions within the agreement until the expiration or end date. |
| **Termination Insights** | |
| Termination for Cause - Notice Period | Refers to provisions setting out the notice period required for a party to terminate a contract for cause. For example, for material breach or other events of default. |
| Termination for Convenience - Notice Period | Refers to provisions setting out the notice period required for a party to terminate a contract for convenience. For example, without a specific reason for doing so and without having to show any breach or default by the other party. |

## Limitation: "Dropdown" attributes (fields) with pre-populated value sets

For attributes (fields) that use dropdowns with pre-populated value sets, only those values are surfaced in your agreements. Agreement processing extracts an attribute value only if it matches one of the values in the dropdown.

When processing cannot locate the value within the agreement, the extracted value is *Other*. For example, if an agreement's payment term is 50 days, the surfaced Payment Term value is *Other*:

This limitation applies to these attributes (fields) and values:

| Attribute (Field) | Value Set |
| --- | --- |
| Assignment (Change of Control) | No, or consent required; Yes, notice required; Yes, restrictions apply; Yes, with conditions |
| Assignment (General) | No, or consent required; Yes; Yes, with conditions |
| Assignment (Termination Rights) | Yes |
| Employment Type | Fixed Term; Full Time; Intern; Part Time |

| Renewal Type | Auto-Renew; Evergreen; Fixed Term; Renewal Option; Unspecified |
