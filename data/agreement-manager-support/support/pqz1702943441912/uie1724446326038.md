# IAM Agreement Type and Field Management

Read about how agreement type management is being used to unify the way Docusign capitalizes on agreement types and associated fields. This topic
answers the question: What is agreement type management and how does it benefit
me?

*Agreement Type and Field Management* helps contract managers and administrators standardize how agreements are classified. When agreements are ingested or uploaded to Agreement Manager, key data points are surfaced based on the identified agreement type. The Agreement Types and Fields features provide a central location to manage your standard and custom agreement types and fields.

Key benefits of agreement type and field management:

- Single source of truth for agreement data:

  - A central place to configure and govern your agreement types and fields.
  - Consistent agreement type and fields definitions across the IAM platform.
- Structured, reusable metadata:
  - Standard fields attached to each agreement type.
  - These fields provide a ready-made data model
    to capture agreements in a structured, comparable
    way.
- Tailored data through custom types and fields:

  - Custom agreement types and fields help to capture the attributes that are not covered by the
    standard, predefined fields.
  - Organizations can use custom types and fields to capture business-specific agreements and attributes (fields).
- Operational consistency and governance:

  - Fields can be added to, edited, or removed from agreement types.
  - Custom types can be created, updated, or removed.
  - Documents can be reprocessed when agreement types and fields change, keeping downstream analytics and workflows accurate over time.

## Surfacing agreement types in documents

Docusign AI models are designed to detect agreement types by considering the title and content of ingested and uploaded files. This includes recognizing synonyms and phrase variations.

For example, "Consulting Agreements" are often seen as "Advisory Agreements" or "Consulting Services Agreements" and "Contractor Agreements" as "Independent Contractor Agreements".

If the AI model does not recognize the agreement type, it is assigned to the "Miscellaneous" type.

If necessary, create a custom agreement type to meet your organizational needs. Once created, you can bulk update agreements to the new agreement type. See these topics for more information:

- [Create a New Agreement Type](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=gke1727282238086.html)
- [Bulk Update the Agreement Type for Selected Agreements](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=uwa1742362757142.html)

Example use cases for your custom agreement types and fields:

- **Technology contracts** may include custom fields for *Hardware specifications*, *Software license terms*, or *Maintenance schedule*.
- **Consulting agreements** may include custom fields for *Engagement scope* and *Deliverables timeline*.
- **Real Estate leases** may include custom fields for *Security deposit amount*, *Pet policy details*, or *Parking options*.

Important:

Customers do not need to consent to AI training to use this feature. When customers create custom
agreement types and fields, their data is used as **input** to the AI model to help generate
results for their use. This process does not involve AI training.

If a customer has given consent to sharing data for AI
training, anonymized data may be used for training Docusign AI models. This may
include anonymized data from custom extractions.

See the [Docusign FAQs for AI](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=fzd1707173174972&topicId=uss1707173279973.html) topic for more information about how

## Permission requirements

By default, all users can view custom agreement types and fields that are surfaced in their agreements. This includes other agreements to which they have access.

Administrators and contract managers create and manage the agreement types and fields for their account. For non-administrator users, this feature requires you to have Organize and manage all agreements access to Agreement Manager agreements and functionality.

See the [Agreement Manager Permissions, Profiles, and Roles](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=eut1712157553977.html&_LANG=enus "Read about the available Agreement Manager permissions, including how users and groups are granted access to Agreement Manager agreements and example roles for each permission.") article to learn more about Agreement Manager access levels.

## Non-agreement types

"Non-agreement" types are used to categorize non-contractual documents that are uploaded and synced to Agreement Manager. Although not related to contractual agreements, these non-agreement types are associated with relevant fields (attributes). When a document is identified as a specific non-agreement type, the associated fields are surfaced within the file.

The supported non-agreement types are:

- Form: often a tool to collect information
- Policy: generally outlines guidelines or rules
- Application: usually a request for consideration
- Certificate: typically a proof of something, like completion
- Report: a summary of findings or data
- Notice: often a communication or information
- Plan: usually outlines strategies or proposals
- Invoice: a request for payment
- Handbook: guidelines or policies
- Summary: condensed information
- Non-agreement/Other

All non-agreement types are associated with the basic attributes or fields. For example, the
Agreement Type, Effective
Date, Parties, and
Title. Depending on the non-agreement
type, additional date and payment fields can also be associated with
the type. For example, the Invoice type
includes fields related to contract value, expiration and execution
dates, and payment terms.

Important: All the custom and standard fields that are associated with an agreement type are surfaced in the relevant documents. However, not all non-agreement types are surfaced in your documents. This includes the custom and standard fields that are associated with the type. These non-agreement types are not surfaced during agreement processing: Certificate, Handbook, Notice, and Policy.

See the [Standard Fields for Agreement and Non-Agreement Types](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=smm1727282189861.html&_LANG=enus "This topic answers the question: What fields are associated with Docusign-provided agreement types? Look up the standard fields that are associated with each agreement and non-agreement type.") topic for a list of fields that are auto-associated
with the standard agreement types.

## Reprocessing historical agreements

As new or updated AI models are released, the previously ingested agreements can be processed again to surface these data points. Depending on the type of new insights that are available, users can choose to:

- Opt in for reprocessing from the Track obligations badge (obligation-related attributes and insights)
- Reprocess all the outdated agreements for the account (new or updated AI model)
- Reprocess selected agreements (custom agreement types and fields)

Note: See the [Agreement Manager Reprocessing Experience](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=con44590246-0f0e-43b4-9692-caadb57335ac.html&_LANG=enus "Learn about the Agreement Manager reprocessing experience, including its purpose, trigger points, and job size considerations.") article to learn more about these reprocessing features.

Processing happens in the background. While it does not impact your current experience, processing may take some time depending on the number and size of agreements. Once started, processing cannot be canceled.
