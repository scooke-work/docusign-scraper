# Agreement Hierarchies

Read about agreement hierarchies, including their purpose, use cases, entry points, and common tasks.

 

When you view agreements as single, standalone documents, you are not seeing the entire picture.
The actual arrangement between the parties is typically much larger and more complex
than a standalone document can show.

This relationship is represented better as a structured collection of legally related
documents, or a contract hierarchy:

![A diagram of an agreement hierarchy for a master services agreement that shows the relationship between the related documents.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/pqz1702943441912/page/images/Contract-hierarchy-generic-example_diagram.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE3OTc4MzIsInNoZWFmIjoicHF6MTcwMjk0MzQ0MTkxMiJ9.NSYxWeJHerx3Wh0i471unl3q2VhrkBnw3r_84ZRpdPI&_LANG=enus "A diagram of an agreement hierarchy for a master services agreement that shows the relationship between the related documents.")

The Hierarchies feature provides a way to create and manage agreement hierarchies. For example, create a hierarchy to capture the relationship between an MSA and supporting agreements. Agreements can be added to or removed from the hierarchy at any time.

## Use cases

Agreement hierarchies provide a way to view and engage with legally related documents. This engagement is critical for understanding the terms and obligations of your agreements. Agreement hierarchies help you to:

**Understand how your agreements evolve over time.** Agreements evolve over time to track changing business needs. For example, expiration dates are extended, pricing schedules are changed, and substantive business terms are updated.

Agreement hierarchies help you to understand how your agreements relate to one another. They can surface amendments and other legally related documents that may change the substantive agreement between the parties.

**Track down legally related documents.** Agreement hierarchies help you to identify
amendments, addenda, statements of work, and other legally related
documents. For example, they can help you locate the legally related
amendments, statements of work, and other documents for an MSA.

Hierarchies save you time when tracking down legally related agreements.

## Common tasks and functionality

Hierarchies are created and managed from the IAM party hub under the Hierarchies tab. From this page, you are prompted to create new hierarchies or update existing ones.

Common tasks and functionality include:

- Create new hierarchies
- Update hierarchy details, such as the title and description
- Add documents to the hierarchy
- Remove documents from the hierarchy
- Update the primary document
- Rearrange the relationships of the documents
- View a document from the Agreement preview page

Restriction: Currently, you cannot delete a hierarchy once created.

## Access to hierarchies

Access to various hierarchy-related functionalities is based on a
user's document-level access. For example:

- **Viewing documents in a hierarchy.** Users can view the documents in a hierarchy if they have Can View, Can Edit, or Can Manage access to the document.

  If you do not have the appropriate document-level access, the documents are not visible in the
  hierarchy.
- **Adding documents.** When creating and updating hierarchies, users can only add documents to which they have Can Edit or Can Manage access.

  If you do not have the appropriate document-level access, you cannot add the agreement to the hierarchy.
- **Editing hierarchies.** Users can only edit a hierarchy if they have Can Edit or Can Manage access to all the documents in the hierarchy.

  If you do not have the appropriate document-level access, you cannot edit the hierarchy. For
  example, you cannot update details, add documents,
  or remove documents.
- **Rearranging document relationships in a hierarchy.** Users must have Can Edit or Can Manage access to all the documents in a hierarchy to rearrange or change the relationship between documents.

  If you do not have the appropriate document-level access, you cannot rearrange or update the document relationships for a hierarchy.
