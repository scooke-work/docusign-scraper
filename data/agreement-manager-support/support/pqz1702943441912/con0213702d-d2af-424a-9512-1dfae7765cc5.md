# Agreement Types Across the IAM Platform

Read about how agreement types are used across the Intelligent Agreement Management (IAM) platform.

In the Docusign IAM platform, an agreement type identifies what kind of document an agreement is, including applicable fields. For example, purchase orders (POs), non-disclosure agreements (NDAs), or master service agreements (MSAs). Each type has its own set of key fields, like parties, term dates, and contract value.

Agreement types and fields are defined once in a central data model and reused across IAM
experiences. For example, they are used in Agreement Preparation, eSignature, CLM,
Agreement Manager, and workflows. This shared model helps teams to:

- Capture consistent agreement data
- Reduce duplicate configuration
- Align downstream reporting

This topic reviews how agreement types are used in the IAM workflow.

## How IAM products use agreement types

Agreement types are used to classify documents and drive which fields are relevant throughout the agreement lifecycle. These fields are centrally stored and can be reused across IAM surfaces. Here's how agreement types are used across the IAM platform:

- **Agreement Preparation:** Agreement types are auto-detected at upload. Based on the identified type, suggested sender fields are surfaced to tag and merge into templates. The types and fields are stored and later used in Agreement Manager. Administrators and contract managers can manage the types and field centrally from Agreement Manager.
- **eSignature:** Templates require users to select an agreement type so that relevant fields are recommended. Envelope- or document-level types and fields provide consistent data points for downstream processes and reporting.
- **Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM):** Defined agreement types and fields are adopted and used in negotiation workflows. CLM-specific attributes that may not be exposed outside of CLM are also supported.
- **Agreement Manager:** Completed agreements with extracted attributes, including the agreement type, are stored in Agreement Manager. Users can filter, sort, and report on agreement data by type or field value. Administrators and contract managers can centrally manage standard and custom agreement types and associated fields. In addition to predefined agreement types, users can add custom types to meet the specific needs of their organization.
- **AI-Assisted Review:** Playbooks are organized by agreement types to ensure that reviews are aligned to type-specific standards. This feature is available to CLM and select IAM plans.
- **Workflow Builder:** Since types are captured at both document and envelope levels, downstream steps can be conditionally driven by agreement type.

Note: IAM platform limits include caps on custom agreement types and fields per account. See the [Custom Extractions](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=yrx1747083411531.html&_LANG=enus "Read about custom extractions, including how these data points bring value to our organization.") article to learn more about the custom extraction and AI detection limits.

## Agreement type lifecycle

At each stage of the agreement type lifecycle, IAM reuses the same agreement type and field definitions. This ensures that AI-assisted detection, workflows, and analytics stay aligned to a single consistent classification.

Consider these phases of the agreement type lifecycle:

![An infographic that shows the lifecycle of an agreement type in the IAM platform.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/pqz1702943441912/page/images/Agreement-type-life-cycle_Docusign-IAM_infographic.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE3OTc0NjIsInNoZWFmIjoicHF6MTcwMjk0MzQ0MTkxMiJ9.hxsJgs780GQ7_DGewJKHrtmC8CITbF_qXMIy48RxeoY&_LANG=enus "An infographic that shows the lifecycle of an agreement type in the IAM platform.")

**Phase 1: Centralize types and fields in Agreement Manager**

In Agreement Manager, administrators centrally manage standard agreement types, create custom types, and define the specific fields that are associated with each category.

**Phase 2: Auto-detect during Agreement Preparation**

When a document is uploaded, the agreement type is detected and sender fields are suggested for tagging in the template. For example, parties, contract value, and expiration date.

**Phase 3: Standardize execution and review**

In eSignature, the selected agreement type aligns the recommended tab fields. The type is captured at both the document and envelope levels to ensure downstream consistency and accurate reporting.

CLM adopts these defined types and fields to drive negotiation workflows. The AI-Assisted Review organizes playbooks by agreement type to standardize reviews and markups.

**Phase 4: Automate management and reporting**

Completed documents are ingested into Agreement Manager with the captured agreement type and extracted attributes. Users can filter, sort, and build reports based on the agreement type.

Since agreement types are captured consistently, they can trigger conditional logic for downstream Workflow Builder workflows. For example, routing approvals or sending renewal alerts.
