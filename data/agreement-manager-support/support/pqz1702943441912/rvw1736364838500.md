# Processing Agreement Manager Agreements

Read about agreement processing and how to opt in for additional processing to surface new insights within your agreements.

When historical and new agreements are imported or uploaded into Agreement Manager, they are processed to surface key data points. These values are surfaced based on the current AI model.

Currently, Agreement Manager supports a number of standard
attributes (fields) related to:

- Assignment and change of control
- Compensation and payment
- Confidentiality
- Governing law, jurisdiction, and dispute resolution
- Limitation of liability
- Objective information
- Renewals
- Term
- Termination

AI insights help you:

- **Drive efficiency:** help increase productivity by reducing manual reviews and data management.
- **Utilize cost-saving opportunities:** identify areas where agreement value can be optimized by uncovering values, like hidden payment terms.
- **Reduce risk:** identify risks within your agreements, such as legal, financial, compliance, strategic, and confidentiality risks.

## Reprocessing historical agreements

As new or updated AI models are released, the previously ingested agreements can be processed again to surface these data points. Depending on the type of new insights that are available, users can choose to:

- Opt in for reprocessing from the Track obligations badge (obligation-related attributes and insights)
- Reprocess all the outdated agreements for the account (new or updated AI model)
- Reprocess selected agreements (custom agreement types and fields)

Note: See the [Agreement Manager Reprocessing Experience](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=con44590246-0f0e-43b4-9692-caadb57335ac.html&_LANG=enus "Learn about the Agreement Manager reprocessing experience, including its purpose, trigger points, and job size considerations.") article to learn more about these reprocessing features.

Processing happens in the background. While it does not impact your current experience, processing may take some time depending on the number and size of agreements. Once started, processing cannot be canceled.

## Processing times and ingestion limits

Historical documents, such as completed agreements from eSignature envelopes, are imported into Agreement Manager automatically. These documents are displayed in the agreement list table on the Completed page.

The number of Docusign historical documents that you can ingest is based
on the IAM plan that was purchased. Limits for historical ingestion:

- 500,000 (North American customers)
- 100,000 (International customers)

The processing time for agreement uploads and ingestion is dependent on the
number of documents being processed. When processing or reprocessing large data
sets, the total processing time can exceed a week.

Depending on how an agreement is uploaded, the processing time can take from hours to days. Consider these examples:

- *Increased processing time due to expanded attribute sets, such as
  Obligation-related attributes.*

  - Processing documents for standard attributes and obligations
    takes longer than processing without obligations.
  - This applies to documents added to Agreement Manager from the
    + Add (+) option.
- *Increased processing time due to congestion in the processing
  queue.*

  - Processing times for documents that are ingested from Docusign eSignature are dependent on the size
    of the data set and the processing queue.
  - This includes the number and size of the jobs already in the
    processing queue.

    Tip: The
    processing queue for documents ingested from eSignature is
    more congested during period close dates, such as month- or
    year-end. You should expect for processing jobs to take
    longer during these times. The typical processing time in
    this scenario is 4–5 days.

## Processing use cases

Consider these examples of when you might want to process agreements for new insights:

- **Updating the agreement type for an agreement:** During data review, the reviewer notices that the surfaced Agreement Type is incorrect and updates the value.
  - Making this type of change automatically initiates a processing job.
  - A dialog box opens. A message informs you about processing the agreement to show specific content for the new agreement type:![A screenshot of the Change agreement type dialog box that shows information about agreement processing.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/pqz1702943441912/page/images/Reprocessing_Change-agreement-type-dialog-box.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE3OTc4NTksInNoZWFmIjoicHF6MTcwMjk0MzQ0MTkxMiJ9.y6-6KSvi7Z3KyRVb4kh9_UUuR3TNzowDaoUhDV61SLQ&_LANG=enus "A screenshot of the Change agreement type dialog box that shows information about agreement processing.")
  - After selecting Change, the dialog box closes. A message states that the agreement type has been changed and the agreement is being processed.
- **Adding new fields to an agreement type:** The contract manager updates an agreement type by adding standard or custom fields to the type. The additional fields are surfaced in any new agreements that are associated with that type. However, the historical agreements that are associated with the agreement type must be processed again.
- **Releasing new attributes (fields) in Agreement Manager:** As new attributes and insights become available, your historical agreements must be reprocessed to surface the new data points. This reprocessing ensures accurate reporting and agreement management.
