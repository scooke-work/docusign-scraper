# Extracted Agreement Insights and Definitions

Look up the names and definitions for the attributes and values that are surfaced in Agreement Manager agreements with AI. This topic answers the question: What data is being extracted from my Agreement Manager agreements?

Attributes and field values are surfaced with Docusign Iris to capture the contractual terms and data. These key data points provide insights into your agreements, and provide greater efficiency and improved risk management.

With these AI-assisted insights, you can:

- **Boost internal productivity:** spend less time manually capturing and tracking data and more time focused on strategic work.
- **Make more informed decisions:** use Docusign Iris to surface critical agreement terms and data points.
- **Expose more potential risks:** monitor compliance with more consistent and reliable agreement records.

Important:

Docusign's latest release includes new attributes (fields)
along with the standard attributes that were previously available. As new
insights are released to support additional use cases, agreements can be
processed again to surface these data points. However, this processing does not
happen automatically.

You must *opt in* to start processing new and existing agreements to surface additional data points. When opting in for processing, your agreements are automatically processed for new insights as they become available.

Review the [Processing Agreement Manager Agreements](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=rvw1736364838500.html) topic to learn more about processing your agreements for new insights.

Use the extracted attributes and values to:

- Filter the agreement list table (Completed Documents view)
- Perform a search query to locate agreements
- Manage renewals (populated with renewal-related attribute values)
- Track payment obligations
- Filter and view agreement reports

CAUTION: The accuracy and appropriateness of AI-assisted insights may vary depending on the agreement language and
customer use case. Always review any attribute values that were surfaced with AI for
accuracy and appropriateness.

Important: Validate all suggested attributes and field values to ensure accuracy. These attributes are used for filtering, searching, renewals and obligation management, and reporting. Review and validate attribute values from the Details pane for a selected agreement.

## Insights by Use Case

Review these sections to learn more about the attributes that comprise various clause types or
use cases.

## Assignment and Change of Control Insights

| **Attribute** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Assignment (Change of Control)\* | Sometimes one or more parties can assign the duties and obligations of the contract to a third party. This assignment might require the consent of the counter-party. This field only concerns assignment upon change of control or merger, or successor in interest. |
| Assignment (General)\* | Sometimes one or more parties can assign the rights, duties, and obligations of an agreement to a third party. This assignment might require the consent of the counter-party. This field only concerns assignment where the nature of the third party is not specified. For example, anything not related to assignment upon change of control or merger. |
| Assignment (Termination Rights)\* | Sometimes one or more parties can assign the duties and obligations of the contract to a third party. If this happens, the contract may allow one or more parties to terminate the agreement early. |

**\*** Included in all IAM plans with Agreement Manager.

## Compensation and Payment Insights

| **Attribute** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Annual Contract Value\* | The total value of all fees due under the agreement during a period of one year, as specified in the agreement. |
| Total Contract Value\* | The total monetary value of an agreement between two or more parties. |
| Late Fees Apply\* | This field indicates whether a party is allowed to charge late fees for payments that are past due. |
| Late Fee %\* | The maximum interest rate that a party is allowed to charge on past due payments. |
| Payment Term\* | This field refers to the general payment terms of an agreement, such as when and how payment for goods or services is expected.  Payment can be due:  - Within a specific period of time - Upon receipt of the invoice - Upon any number of other provisions  Important: For attributes (fields) that use dropdowns with pre-populated values, like Payment Term, only those values are surfaced in your agreements. Agreement processing extracts an attribute only if it matches one of the values in the dropdown. See the [Language and Regional Support](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=xvp1729795957126.html&_LANG=enus "Read about language support and regional availability for Agreement Manager AI models and features.") topic to learn more about this limitation. |
| Price Cap Increase %\* | The maximum amount by which a party can raise their prices when the term is renewed, as a percentage of the previous price. |

**\*** Included in all IAM plans with Agreement Manager.

## Governing Law, Jurisdiction, and Dispute Resolution Insights

| **Attribute** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Governing Law\* | Refers to the laws of a specific place or the system of law that is applied in the event of a legal dispute. |
| Jurisdiction\* | Refers to the location where any legal disputes related to the agreement are tried. The venue for disagreements is the name or location of the courts where the trial must take place. This field also encompasses locations for arbitration, if it is laid out in the contract. |

**\*** Included in all IAM plans with Agreement Manager.

## Objective Information Insights

| **Attribute** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Agreement Type\* | Refers to the classification of an agreement according to references in its title and its subject matter. For example, Master Services Agreement (MSA), Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDA), and Statement of Work (SOW). |
| Execution Date\* | Refers to the date on which the contract became a legally binding agreement that was signed by all the necessary parties.  If the execution date is missing from the document and all parties have signed it, the date defaults to the latest signature date. |
| Languages\* | Identifies which languages are present in the agreement. |
| Line of Business | This field indicates the line of business to which the agreement most closely aligns. For example, Sales, Procurement, Human Resources, and unspecified. |
| NDA Type\* | A Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) is described as: One-way or Mutual.  - Mutual (or Bilateral) NDAs are where both parties are exchanging information. - One way (or Unilateral) NDAs are where one party is receiving information and the other party is   providing it. |
| Parties\* | Refers to a provision naming the individuals, organizations, or entities that enter into a legally binding agreement. |
| Party Role | This field identifies which party is the buyer as opposed to the seller within the context of an agreement. |
| Status | This field provides a look at where the agreement is within its life cycle. It indicates whether the agreement is active, inactive, effective soon, expiring soon, and renewing soon. |
| Title\* | The title of the document. It is usually at the top of the page and describes the subject of the agreement. |

**\*** Included in all IAM plans with Agreement Manager.

## Renewal Insights

| **Attribute** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| Renewal Type\* | Comprised of these data extractions: Auto Renew, Evergreen, Fixed Term, and Renewal Option. |
| Auto Renew (Renewal Type)\* | Renewal type. Refers to provisions that state an agreement is renewed automatically at the end of its term. |
| Evergreen (Renewal Type)\* | Renewal type. Refers to clauses that contemplate a perpetual continuation of the agreement until a party takes action to terminate it or a certain condition is fulfilled. |
| Fixed Term (Renewal Type)\* | Renewal type. Refers to an agreement that is effective for a specified period. |
| Renewal Option (Renewal Type)\* | Renewal type. Refers to provisions that set out a party’s option to extend the term of the contract. |
| Renewal Term\* | Refers to a fixed period for which an agreement is extended after its initial term expires. These provisions set out:  - Duration of an auto-renewal period (Term: Renewal Automatic) - Party's option to extend an agreement for a specified term (Term: Renewal Option) - Additional period of time that an agreement will be extended beyond its initial term (Extension Period) |
| Extension Period\* | Refers to provisions that set out the additional period of time (duration) that an agreement will be extended beyond its initial term. |
| Renewal Notice Date\* | Refers to the date by which one or more parties must provide official notice to continue an agreement. |
| Renewal Notice Period\* | Refers to the period of time that is required for a party to give notice of their intent to renew an agreement. NonRenewal Notice Period refers to the period of time that is required for a party to give notice of their intent not to renew an agreement. |

**\*** Included in all IAM plans with Agreement Manager.
