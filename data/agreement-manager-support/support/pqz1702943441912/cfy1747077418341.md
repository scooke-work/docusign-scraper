# Agreement Type Definitions

Look up definitions for Docusign-provided agreement types.

Docusign provides a number of standard agreement types. These agreement types can be customized to meet your organization's unique use cases. Review the agreement type definitions to recognize the purpose and usage of each type.

## A

| **Agreement Type** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Addendum** | A legal agreement that is added to an existing contract. Addendums are additions that are made to an agreement after it has been completed or executed.  Addendums can provide additional information or clarification, or make changes to certain terms or provisions in the original agreement.  An addendum modifies the original agreement. It is not a standalone document. Once signed by all involved parties, the addendum becomes part of the original document. |
| **Amendment** | A legal agreement that details an official modification to an already existing agreement or legal document.  Amendments are used to modify the original document without completely rewriting it. Once signed by all involved parties, the amendment becomes part of the original document. |
| **Appendix** | A supplementary document that is appended to a legal agreement or larger document. Appendices provide additional support to the main content of an agreement or document. |
| **Application** | A formal request for approval, service, or consideration. Typically applications are submitted to an authority, institution, or organization.  Examples of applications include: Employment applications, loan applications, bids, and claims. |
| **Attachment** | A document or group of documents that are physically added to a contract post-signature. Attachments can also be included by reference in the body of the agreement.  Attachments can include plans, drawings, specifications, or other information that is necessary for understanding or fulfilling the agreement. |

COIs typically include:   - Proof of insurance coverage - Policy information - Certificate holder - Exclusions and limitations - Notification requirements. |

| **Contractor Agreement** | A legal contract between a business or individual and an independent contractor. Contractor agreements outline the terms and conditions of their working relationship. |
| **Credit Card Agreement** | A legal contract between a cardholder and a credit card issuer. Credit card agreements set forth the terms and conditions governing the use of the credit card. |

## E

| **Agreement Type** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Employment Separation Agreement** | A legal contract between employers and employees that outlines the terms and conditions for the termination of employment.  This agreement typically addresses various aspects of the separation, such as:   - Severance pay - Continuation of benefits - Confidentiality obligations - Non-disparagement obligations - Other post-employment obligations |
| **Engagement Letter** | A legal agreement between clients and service providers that outlines the terms and conditions of a professional services agreement.  Examples of service providers include accountants, lawyers, or consultants. |

| **Exhibit** | A document that is attached to the end of a contract to provide additional information or evidence.  Exhibits support the claims made in the main documents or in ancillary documents and may have an evidentiary purpose. |

## L

| **Agreement Type** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Lease** | A legally binding agreement where one party conveys land, property, services, or privileges in exchange for the other party making regular payments. Payments can also include an exchange for something else of value.  The most commonly occurring type of lease is between landlords (owners) and tenants. Landlords grant their tenants the right to use and occupy a piece of real property. In return, the tenants make regular payments (rents) for the duration of the lease.  In lease agreements for real property, the tenant is often referred to as the lessee and the landlord as the lessor. |
| **Letter of Intent (LOI)** | A document that outlines the preliminary agreement between two parties for a future or proposed contract.  An LOI is not legally binding. It is a written statement of the key terms and conditions of the proposed agreement.  LOIs typically precede the execution of a formal contract. |
| **License** | A legal agreement that grants permission and sets terms and conditions for individuals or organizations to use, reproduce, or distribute a product, service, or intellectual property. For example, software, music, or patents.  A license can be exclusive or non-exclusive and can be granted for a specific period of time or indefinitely.  In license agreements, the individual or entity who owns or controls the rights to the product, service, or intellectual property is the licensor. The individual or entity who is granted permission to use, reproduce, or distribute the product, service, or intellectual property is the licensee . |
| **Loan** | A legal contract between lenders (the party providing the loan) and borrowers (the party receiving the loan). These agreements outline the terms and conditions under which the loan is made. |

## M

| **Agreement Type** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Marketing** | A legally binding contract between two or more parties that outlines the terms and conditions for promoting, advertising, or selling a product, service, or brand.  Marketing agreements typically define the roles, responsibilities, compensation, and expectations of each involved party. |
| **Master Services Agreement (MSA)** | A legal contract between two parties, typically clients and service providers, that establishes the terms and conditions under which services are provided to the client.  MSAs are a type of framework agreement that establishes a long-term business relationship between the parties. These agreements are used for ongoing, repetitive services or for a series of related projects. |
| **Memorandum of Understanding (MOI)** | A legally non-binding document that outlines the terms and understanding of an agreement between two or more parties.  Although not legally binding, an MOI signals the willingness of the parties to move forward. The parties can act and negotiate in good faith to reach a contractual agreement.  MOIs are used to establish a framework for a formal agreement and often serves as a starting point for negotiations. |
| **Miscellaneous (Other)** | Legal agreements or non-legal documents that fall outside of the active agreement types, including custom agreement types. |

## R

| **Agreement Type** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Release/Waiver** | A legally binding contract where one party (the releasor) agrees to waive or relinquish legal claims, rights, or liabilities against another party (the releasee).  Releases or waivers often include compensation, a settlement, or other considerations in exchange for relinquishing rights to further action. |
| **Renewal (Agreement/Notice)** | A legally binding contract that extends the duration of an existing agreement, often under the same or modified terms.  Renewal agreements or notices ensure the continuation of the rights, obligations, and benefits between the involved parties. They extend the existing agreement without requiring a new contract. |

## S

| **Agreement Type** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Service Level Agreement (SLA)** | A contract between service providers and customers that defines the level of service that the provider will deliver. SLAs typically include:   - Responsibilities - Measurable performance standards - Penalties for service failures - Credits for services that exceed expectations - Service hours - Resolution times for service requests. |
| **Services Agreement** | A legal contract between service providers and clients that outlines the terms and conditions of the services being provided.  Services agreements help ensure that both parties understand their responsibilities and obligations, and can provide a framework for resolving any disputes that may arise. |
| **Statement of Work (SOW)** | A contract that provides a detailed description of the specific tasks, activities, and deliverables for a project or a piece of work.  SOWs typically include:   - Scope of work to complete - Objectives and outcomes to achieve - Timeframe for completion - Project requirements |
| **Stock Purchase Agreement** | A legally binding contract that outlines the terms and conditions under which sellers transfer the ownership of stock (shares) in a company to buyers. |
| **Subscription (Agreement for Software or Services)** | A *software subscription agreement* (SSA) is a legally binding contract between software providers (licensors) and customer (subscribers). SSAs grant customers access to use the software on a subscription basis.  A *service subscription agreement* (SSA) is a legally binding contract between service providers and customers. SSAs outline the terms under which the customer subscribes to and receives ongoing, recurring services. |
| **Summary** | A concise and structured overview of the key points, main ideas, or essential details of a larger text, discussion, or event.  Summaries present the most important information in a clear, brief, and easily understandable format without unnecessary details or personal opinions. |
| **Supplemental Document** | A document that provides additional information or materials that support or elaborate on the main document.  These documents or materials are used to supplement the main document and to provide more detailed information. |
| **Supply/Distribution** | The key difference between a supply agreement and a distribution agreement is that in a supply agreement, the buyer receives goods or services for their own use, consumption, or manufacturing. In a distribution agreement, a distributor purchases the goods with the intention of reselling them.  - A *supply agreement* is a legal contract between a supplier and a buyer that sets out the terms and conditions under which the supplier will provide certain goods, materials, or services to the buyer over a specified period. - A *distribution agreement* is a legal contract between a manufacturer or supplier and a distributor that typically specifies the products or services that the distributor is authorized to sell. The contract also specifies the territory in which they can sell the product or service and any exclusivity provisions that may apply. |

## T

| **Agreement Type** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Termination (Agreement/Notice)** | *Termination agreements* are legally binding contracts that formally end an existing agreement between two or more parties.  These agreements outline the terms, conditions, and obligations related to ending a contractual relationship. They ensure that all understand their rights and responsibilities post-termination.  *Termination notices* are formal, written documents that notify a party of the intent to end a contract, agreement, or relationship.  These notices specify the termination's effective date, the reason (if applicable), and any final obligations or required actions before the agreement officially ends. |
| **Terms & Conditions** | A document that outlines the rights, responsibilities, and obligations of both the customer and the company providing the product or service.  Terms and conditions also set out the rules regarding any limitations or restrictions on the use of a product or service.  Companies establish these rules and guidelines to govern the use of their products or services. |
