# Agreement Manager Reprocessing Experience

Learn about the Agreement Manager reprocessing experience, including its purpose, trigger points,
and job size considerations.

When organizations are first onboarded to Agreement Manager, their historical documents are ingested and processed. During processing, attributes and field values are surfaced based on the current AI model. When new or updated models are available, the previously ingested and uploaded documents are not reprocessed for the new insights.

The new or updated AI models include a number of updates that require reprocessing:

- **New extraction sets:** sets of related activities such as Human Resources (HR) and Obligations
- **New extractions:** single attributes, such as Line of Business and Party Role
- **New languages:** expanded support for new languages, such as Brazilian-Portuguese, French, German, Japanese, and Spanish (Mexico, Spain)
- **Accuracy improvements**
- **Bug fixes**

Tip: Reprocess your historical documents when new custom agreement types and fields are available. For example, select specific agreements to reprocess for a new agreement type.

Users can only benefit from these updates by reprocessing their agreements for the latest AI models.

## Importance of reprocessing

During processing, key data points are extracted based on the original AI model that was used during onboarding. These extractions populate the agreement insights that you use to manage your agreements, renewals, and obligations.

When new or updated AI models are released, your agreement insights become outdated and unreliable - possibly missing key data points.

Reprocessing is necessary to ensure that your searches, filters, and reports provide reliable and accurate agreement insights.

## Types of reprocessing experiences

Depending on the type of insights and enhancements that are available with the new AI model, users can choose to:

- Opt in for reprocessing from the Track obligations badge (obligation-related attributes and insights)
- Reprocess all the outdated agreements for the account (new or updated AI model)
- Reprocess selected agreements (custom and standard data agreement types and fields)

## Job size considerations

The reprocessing job happens in the background. While it does not impact your current experience, processing times vary depending on the size of the job. Other items to consider:

| Job size | Consideration |
| --- | --- |
| Less than 1,000 documents | Reprocessing jobs with less than 1,000 documents are sent to the "live" queue. The "live" queue is used for manually initiated processing jobs, such as agreement uploads and imports. |
| More than 1,000 documents | Reprocessing jobs with more than 1,000 documents are sent to the "historical" queue. The "historical" queue is used for larger jobs, such as batch processing and onboarding new organizations. |
| More than 25,000 documents | Reprocessing jobs with more than 25,000 documents are batch processed. The job is separated into batches of 25,000 each and sent to the "historical" queue. Note: Batch processing jobs have a built-in time delay of 2 days. |

Once started, processing cannot be canceled.

## Reprocessing FAQs

Review these frequently asked questions to learn more about agreement reprocessing:

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Can I reprocess agreements? | Yes. Any user on the account can trigger reprocessing jobs. |
| What if some of my agreements are up to date? | The reprocessing job automatically skips any documents that are currently up to date. |
| Will my existing agreement data be overwritten? | The reprocessing job may result in the surfaced data points being recalculated and overwritten:  - For values that were manually entered or marked as   "accepted", the reprocessing job does *not*   overwrite or alter these values. - For fields that contain multiple values, the   reprocessing job may surface additional values -   even if the fields were "accepted". |
| Are my agreement sets impacted by reprocessing? | If you use smart filters with your agreement sets, the reprocessing *could* change which documents are stored in a set. For example, an agreement was classified as a non-disclosure agreement (NDA) and added to a set based on a smart filter. If reprocessing results in the agreement's type being changed to a master service agreement (MSA), the agreement will be removed from its current set.  Important: If an agreement was manually added to an agreement set, the agreement remains in the set even if the surfaced values are changed during reprocessing. |
