# Custom Obligation Types

Read about custom obligation types, including use cases, permissions, capabilities, and entry points.

Custom obligation types are unique categories of obligations that are tailored to meet organizational or departmental needs. These obligations can relate to compliance, performance, or other critical contract aspects.

Docusign provides a set of standard obligation types, however, you can create custom types that more accurately categorize your obligations. These custom obligation types provide:

- **Enhanced compliance and risk management:** Track compliance and adherence to legal and regulatory requirements.
- **Improved performance training:** Monitor and measure the performance of involved parties against their commitments.
- **Streamlined contract management:** Organize, track, and manage various aspects of an agreement.
- **Tailored reporting:** Customize and generate reports that are relevant to your specific use case and obligation type.
- **Assists strategic decision-making:** Use insights and data to support strategic decisions, such as renegotiating terms, extending contracts, or terminating agreements.

## Example roles and use cases

This feature is primarily used by contract managers, and possibly administrators. Here are a few examples of how various roles are using obligation types:

- **Procurement Managers**

  Uses standard and custom obligation types to assess the health and performance of vendors:

  - Track obligations ahead of due dates to determine the appropriate actions to take.
  - Visualize that payment obligations are being met to avoid missed payments and penalty fees.
  - Report on obligations to ensure that the organization is taking advantage of negotiated terms, discounts, and rebates.
- **Contract Managers**

  Creates custom obligation types and manages obligations, including assignments and reminders:

  - Monitor and track obligations to ensure fulfillment
  - Assign obligations to relevant team members
  - Communicate obligation requirements
- **Legal Operations Manager**

  Uses obligation types to flag high-risk obligations and follow up on services levels and renegotiation rights:

  - Locate relevant documents and obligations more quickly
  - Track obligations in a systematic and accurate way
  - Ensure that the appropriate stakeholders have access to important obligations

## Permission requirements

Obligation type management requires the Organize and manage all agreements permission. This permission is the highest access level for Agreement Manager users and includes creating and managing obligation types.

See the [Agreement Manager Permissions, Profiles, and Roles](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=eut1712157553977.html&_LANG=enus "Read about the available Agreement Manager permissions, including how users and groups are granted access to Agreement Manager agreements and example roles for each permission.") topic to learn more about Agreement Manager access levels.

Use this page to manage the types of obligations in your account:

- Create new (custom) obligation types
- Rename custom obligation types
- Hide or unhide custom and Docusign-provided (standard) obligation types

  Note: When an obligation type is hidden, it is hidden from the:
  - Type dropdown on the Obligations pane (Agreement preview page)
  - Obligation Type dropdown on the Obligations dashboard

  If an agreement contains a hidden obligation type, those obligations are still visible. Existing obligation instances with the hidden type are also visible.
