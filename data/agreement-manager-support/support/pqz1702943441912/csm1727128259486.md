# Obligation Management

Read about obligation management, including how to use this functionality to capture, track, and fulfill your agreement obligations. This topic answers the question: How can I find and track my agreement obligations?

Obligation management helps contract managers to capture, track, and fulfill their contract
commitments. Obligations are the actions or commitments that one or more
parties agree to fulfill as part of an agreement. They are made up of
attributes and extracted contract data that is surfaced from uploaded and
ingested documents.

## Primary user role

Obligation management is primarily used by contract managers. This is a broad persona that
includes procurement managers, category managers, and legal
operations. Consider these pain points for each role:

- Identifying important obligations can be tedious and time-consuming
- No easy way to monitor and collaborate on obligations
- Lack of fulfillment tracking often leads to lost revenue

## Obligation management process

Contractual data points, or attributes, are surfaced either manually or with AI and provide an in-depth view of the obligations contained in all agreement types. With these insights, contract managers can capture, track, and fulfill obligations from signature to expiration.

The obligation management process includes these steps:

1. **Capture obligations:** obligations are surfaced with AI or are created manually by contract
   managers.
2. **Monitor obligations:** obligations are tracked at the agreement- and
   party-level.
3. **Subscribe to email report notifications:** set up one-time or recurring
   email reports to get notified of upcoming due dates.
4. **Report on obligations:** the Obligations dashboard
   provides a visual summary of agreement obligations and access to a variety
   of reports.

## Obligations surfaced with AI

During processing, key data points are surfaced in your agreements, including payment, renewal,
and termination obligations. Each obligation is made up of a number
of attributes (fields) that are also surfaced in your
agreements.

Note: The obligations listed
in the table are extracted from your documents if they
exist within the document.

| Obligation type | Obligation name | Extracted attributes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Payment | One-Time Payment | Obligation Name (One-Time Payment), Type (Payment), Status, Payment Value, Due Date, Frequency (One Time), Obligated Party, Discounts, and Repercussions |
| Deposit Payment | Obligation Name (Deposit Payment), Type (Payment), Status, Payment Value, Due Date, Frequency (One Time), Obligated Party, Discount, and Repercussions |
| Renewal | Renewal Notice | Obligation Name (Renewal Notice), Type (Renewal), Status, Notice Date, Notice Period, Obligated Party, and Repercussions |
| Termination | Termination for Cause Notice | Obligation Name (Termination for Cause Notice), Type (Termination), Status, Notice Date, Notice Period, Obligated Party, and Repercussions |
| Termination for Convenience Notice | Obligation Name (Termination for Convenience Notice), Type (Termination), Status, Notice Date, Notice Period, Obligated Party, and Repercussions |

Important:

Obligations are surfaced in English (U.S.) agreements only. The AI model that processes obligations is run from a U.S. server and can only process English agreements in this region.

If your language and region are not supported, manually create the obligations to include this information in dashboards, reports, and other insights.

## Supported obligation types (manually surfaced)

A number of obligation types can be manually created from an agreement:![A snippet of the Agreement preview page that highlights the Create Obligation option.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/pqz1702943441912/page/images/Obligation-mgmt_Create-obligation-example.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE3OTcxMzcsInNoZWFmIjoicHF6MTcwMjk0MzQ0MTkxMiJ9.xebPLFG5xouk8WgT_edHtnaQzKSLJ1KC31tOCOPX4Wk&_LANG=enus)

- Compliance obligations: specific rules, laws, regulations, or policies that an organization is legally or ethically required to follow.
- Confidentiality obligations: duty to use confidential information only for its intended purpose.
- Data breach obligations: legal requirement for an organization to notify affected individuals and relevant authorities when a data breach occurs.
- Escalation obligations: provisions that allow for increases in wages or prices if economic conditions change throughout the contract.
- Indemnification obligations: agreements that require one party to compensate another party for incurred losses.
- Insurance obligations: duties or
  responsibilities that either the insurer or the policyholder
  has under an insurance contract.
- Limitation of liability obligations: clauses that restrict the amount of damages one party can claim from another in the event of a breach or legal issue.
- Miscellaneous obligations: types of documents used in the financial systems of some government agencies, or a type of payment made by the government.
- Notification obligations: legal or contractual requirements for one party to inform another party about specific events, circumstances, or actions.
- Payment obligations: requirement to make payments on a transaction or debt.
- Renewal notification obligations: legal requirements to send a clear and timely notice to customers about their upcoming renewals (that will automatically renew unless canceled).
- Service level agreements obligations: commitments made by a service provider to a customer, outlining the specific standards of service they will deliver. Often these obligations include details like response times and resolution procedures.
- Subcontracting obligations: the legal
  responsibility an organization (subcontractor) assumes when
  they agree to perform a part of a larger project for another
  organization (main contractor).
- Termination obligations: clauses that
  require one party to provide advance notice to another party
  before terminating the contract due to a specific breach or
  violation.
- Warranty obligations: legal
  commitments made by a seller or manufacturer to repair,
  replace, or provide compensation for a product that fails to
  meet certain quality standards.

## Obligation statuses

Obligation statuses provide a quick look at where the obligation is within its life cycle.

![A snippet of an obligation report that highlights the Obligation Status filter and column.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/pqz1702943441912/page/images/Obligation-mgmt_Obligation-Status_higlighted.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE3OTcxMzcsInNoZWFmIjoicHF6MTcwMjk0MzQ0MTkxMiJ9.xebPLFG5xouk8WgT_edHtnaQzKSLJ1KC31tOCOPX4Wk&_LANG=enus)

Each obligation has a status. These statuses are calculated based on the data points that are surfaced in your agreements:

| Status | Calculation explanation |
| --- | --- |
| Active | This status is applied to obligations that do not have a due date. Active obligations are extracted from active and in-effect agreements:  - Effective date is equal to or before the current date (the agreement is "active") - Expiration date is equal to or after the current date (the agreement has not "expired") |
| Scheduled | This status is applied to obligations with due dates that are greater than 30 days in the future from the current date. Scheduled obligations are extracted from active and in-effect agreements:  - Effective date is equal to or before the current date (the agreement is "active") - Expiration date is equal to or after the current date (the agreement has not "expired") |
| Due soon | This status is applied to obligations with due dates that are equal to or less than 30 days in the future from the current date. Due soon obligations are extracted from active and in-effect agreements:  - Effective date is equal to or before the current date (the agreement is "active") - Expiration date is equal to or after the current date (the agreement has not "expired") |
| Passed | This status is applied to obligations with due dates that are before the current date. |
| Inactive | This status is applied to obligations where the associated agreements are either not yet effective or expired:  - Effective date is after the current date (the agreement and associated   obligations are not yet effective) Note: If an   obligation has a due date but the associated   agreement is not yet effective, the obligation   status is   Inactive. - Expiration date is before the current date and the obligations do not have a due date value Note: If the obligation is missing a due date and the associated agreement is expired, the obligation status is Inactive. |

Important: The obligation status is calculated continuously based on the extracted agreement data. When editing an obligation's status, only the possible status options are displayed - based on the ongoing calculations.![A snippet of the Edit Obligation pane that highlights the Status dropdown.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/pqz1702943441912/page/images/Obligation-mgmt_Edit-Obligation_Status-dropdown_highlighted.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE3OTcxMzcsInNoZWFmIjoicHF6MTcwMjk0MzQ0MTkxMiJ9.xebPLFG5xouk8WgT_edHtnaQzKSLJ1KC31tOCOPX4Wk&_LANG=enus)

The remaining statuses are not calculated. Users are responsible for marking obligations with these statuses:

- Dependent: the obligation is dependent on another event happening (circumstantial obligation)
- Fulfilled: the obligation has been completed (the obligated party fulfilled the obligation)
- Unfulfilled: the obligation has been breached (the obligated party failed to fulfill the obligation)

## Entry points

There are three primary ways to access your obligation information:

- **Obligations dashboard:** located under the Reports menu, the Obligations dashboard displays obligations by due date and type.

  You can filter the obligations list, subscribe to notifications, and download or save obligation reports.

  See the [Obligations Dashboard](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=qal1734545813030.html&_LANG=enus "Read about the Obligations dashboard, including available reports and filter options.") to learn more about the obligations dashboard reports.
- **Party profile - Obligations tab:** located in the party
  hub, the Obligations tab shows a list
  of obligations that are tied to a specific party or alias
  group.

  See the [View Obligations for Selected Parties and Groups](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=brh1742456493450.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to view all the obligations that are tied to a specific party or alias group.") to learn more about viewing obligations from the
  party hub.
- **Agreement preview - Obligations pane:** located on the
  Agreement preview page, the
  Obligations pane displays
  obligations that are associated with a selected agreement.

  The pane displays details for both AI-assisted
  and manually created obligations, including the
  name, type, status, value, and frequency.

  See
  the [Complete a Data Review for Extracted Obligations](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=fwg1749056504311.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to complete a data review to verify the obligations that were surfaced in your agreements.") to learn
  more about reviewing obligations from the
  Agreement preview page.

## Report notification subscriptions

The Obligations dashboard consists of various charts. When you select any of the charts, a detailed report view opens. From this view, select Subscribe and complete the fields in the Create subscription dialog box.

![A screenshot of the Create subscription dialog box that shows the subscription configuration fields.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/pqz1702943441912/page/images/Reports_Create-Subscription.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE3OTcxMzcsInNoZWFmIjoicHF6MTcwMjk0MzQ0MTkxMiJ9.xebPLFG5xouk8WgT_edHtnaQzKSLJ1KC31tOCOPX4Wk&_LANG=enus)

After completing the necessary fields, select Schedule. You will now receive report notifications about the obligations in the selected report. See the [Subscribe to Receive Report Notifications](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=wfw1734545937270.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to set up one-time or recurring email reports for yourself or others to receive notifications about your obligations, renewals, or agreements.") to learn more about subscribing to receive email notifications for your obligations.
