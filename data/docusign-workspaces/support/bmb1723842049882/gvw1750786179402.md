# Identity Verification

Read about how Workspaces provide identity verification so that you can ensure individuals are authorized to access documents and agreements within a workspace.

Workspaces provides you with the ability to verify the identity of your clients per regulatory mandates, such as Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements. Many financial services companies must verify a participant’s identity before the participant accesses a workspace to protect documents and agreements within a transaction.

Table 1. ID Verification methods

| Verification Method | Description | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Verification of government-issued IDs for simple and advanced signatures﻿. | Captures recipients' government-issued ID and verifies their identity. | - Employee onboarding - Verification of customer's identity - Bank account opening - Loan Agreements - Patient consent forms |
| Liveness detection for ID verification of government-issued IDs for simple and advanced signatures﻿. | Adds liveness detection to the standard verification of government-issued IDs. Recipients must scan their ID. Next, they must record a selfie video and conduct liveness challenges to verify their identity. | - Account opening under AML (Anti-Money Laundering) and KYC (Know   Your Customer) requirements - Access to personal medical records |
| Verification of government-issued IDs for European qualified signatures. | Adds liveness detection and manual transaction review to the standard government-issued ID verification method. This method targets customers who want to generate European qualified electronic signatures (QES). | - Signature of notarized legal documents - Digital access to government services - Temporary worker contracts - Commercial lease contracts |
| Verification Using CLEAR﻿. | Verifies identity of U.S recipients using their CLEAR account information. | - Loan applications - Patient onboarding - Customer onboarding |
| Knowledge-based authentication (KBA)﻿ | Checks recipients' identity with questions based on their public records, such as their home address. | - Medical insurance claim submission - Digital consent signature - Loan agreements - Retirement investment agreements - Bank account changes |
| Phone Authentication | Authenticates recipients with a one-time passcode (OTP) allowing them to continue the process. | - Board meeting minutes - Employment agreements - Non-Disclosure Agreements |

A workspace owner can configure the workspace to require ID verification for a participant to access the workspace. The owner can also choose a specific verification method.

The certificate of completion is updated when an envelope within the workspace includes identity verification that uses the same configuration used by the IDV required in the workspace.
