# The Signing Experience

Read about the signing experience and how the signer signs and completes your
electronic signature transaction.

You've prepared your envelope and sent it for signature. What is the signing experience
like? How do your documents display to recipients? Where do the messages and fields that
you added show up, and how does the signer complete the electronic signature
process?

## Typical signing scenario

Let's walk through a typical signing scenario:

## Scenario – Signing a Document

You manage a small apartment building and have a new prospective tenant, Grant
England. You send Grant a rental agreement for signature and a parking memo to
acknowledge. With a waiting list for the unit, you need the agreement completed
within five days. To ensure this, you set a reminder for Grant every day and an
expiration date for the document in five days. You also explain the deadline to
Grant in the document message.

The building's owner, Lissa Burnky, wants copies of all completed rental agreements.
You add Lissa as a copy-only recipient to your document. You use the Sign in Order
feature so that Lissa receives the document only after Grant signs it. You add a
private note to Lissa explaining that Grant passed the credit and reference
checks.

Here are the steps in the signing experience. The screenshots show you in detail what your
recipients see.

1. Your prospective tenant, Grant, receives an email notification from you through Docusign.
