# Protect Your Shared Templates

Read about setting passwords to protect shared templates.

When you share a template, you grant the selected users or groups the right to edit the
template, share it with others, and copy it, provided the users have the necessary permissions
(template create or share). To protect a shared template, you can set a password or lock parts
of the template.

- Set a password. You can protect your shared template from
  being modified by other users by  [setting a
  password](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=xry1643227563338&topicId=xwo1578456395432.html&_LANG=enus "Template passwords protect against editing. If the template is shared with other users, for them to edit the shared template, they must provide the password."). Then, if the template is shared with other users, they must provide the
  password to edit, share, or copy the shared template.

  Administrators on your account have full access to all templates created within the account. They can edit templates and modify
  sharing settings, regardless of the password setting.
- Lock template elements. You can also apply settings to
  [limit the types of changes](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=xry1643227563338&topicId=gsz1578456308195.html&_LANG=enus "Use template and recipient modification settings to limit the types of changes that can be made when your templates are used to send a document. Use the Sender Permission settings to protect fields on a template from being changed or deleted.") that other users can
  make when using the template to send an envelope. You can control how others use the
  template by locking the recipient information, the email message to all recipients, private
  messages to individual recipients, and the brand.
