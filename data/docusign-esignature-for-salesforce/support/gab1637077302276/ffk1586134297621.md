# Step 3: Paste Anchor Text Fields Into Your Word Document

Learn to paste your Salesforce anchor text fields into a Microsoft Word document.

Use this procedure to paste your Salesforce anchor
text fields into a Microsoft Word document.

Tip:
[This video explains the step](https://players.brightcove.net/6118377982001/default_default/index.html?videoId=6248882103001).

1. Select Copy next to the first anchor text field.
2. Paste the anchor text field in the correct spot of your Word template.

   Note: In this example, the
   administrator pastes the Name field where the customer name
   should display. If a user generates a contract for Acme, Co., that
   name replaces the Name anchor text.
   ![A screen capture displays an administrator pasting anchor text into a Word document.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/gab1637077302276/page/images/Copy-and-paste-data-fields-to-Word.gif?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODIxNTc5MDIsInNoZWFmIjoiZ2FiMTYzNzA3NzMwMjI3NiJ9.yrSLxd4_y9DgW05ZhWBY7UuAD3Ft2vXFqhh1aGp3h1A&_LANG=enus "A screen capture displays an administrator pasting anchor text into a Word document.")
3. Repeat this process for each anchor text field.
4. Save the updated Word template to your device.

You have completed step three. Move to [Step 4: Upload the Word Document to Your Gen Template](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=gab1637077302276&topicId=gxl1586134297397.html&_LANG=enus "Learn to upload your Word template to use in your Docusign Gen template.").
