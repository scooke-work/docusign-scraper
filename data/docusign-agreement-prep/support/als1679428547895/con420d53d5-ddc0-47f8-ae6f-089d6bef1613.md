# Sender and Signer Fields

This topic explains the roles of sender and signer fields in templates used in Agreement Preparation.

Sender and signer fields are two key types of data placeholders used when building a template in Agreement Preparation.

- **Sender Fields:**

  Sender fields are essential for ensuring that agreements are populated with the correct data provided by the preparer. They are used to customize the generated agreement with accurate data, which can include information pulled from external systems like Salesforce.

  - These fields are placeholders for **data provided by the preparer (Sender Role)** when an agreement is generated.
  - Agreement Fields are the sender fields that are associated with the Agreement Type. Each agreement type has a set of standard fields, and also includes any user-defined custom fields. Agreement fields are account-based and available to all account users with the appropriate permissions.

    Note: Agreement types and fields are managed in Agreement Manager by users with the appropriate permissions. For details, see [this help topic](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=pqz1702943441912&topicId=ypm1727282359686.html&_LANG=enus). Agreement Manager is available for Docusign IAM plans only.
- **Signer Fields (Recipient Fields):**

  Signer fields are crucial for enabling recipients to complete or take action on the agreement.

  - These fields are assigned to the **Recipient Role (Signer)**, who is the participant in the agreement.
  - They are the **standard signing fields** for the signer to complete or take action on.
