# “There Were Some Problems with Your Document Generation” Displays When I Generate an Agreement

Learn to fix issues with Gen when the “There Were Some Problems with Your Document
Generation” error displays.

The “There Were Some Problems With Your Document Generation” error displays for many
reasons. Use the following resolutions to fix this issue:

## Resolution 1: Check If the User Is Active

Cause: If a Gen user has not activated their account, the document
generation process fails. To have Active status, users must create a Docusign password, and authorize the Gen app upon first use.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. [Open Docusign Apps Launcher as
   an administrator.](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=etv1586134206174.html&_LANG=enus "Learn to open the Docusign Apps Launcher for Salesforce package in your organization.")
2. Select User Management in the left column.
3. Check the Status column.
4. Inform Pending users that they must [activate their user accounts](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=ymi1586134213877.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to activate your Docusign CLM, Gen, Negotiate, or eSignature for Salesforce user.").

   You have completed the process. When the user completes activation, they can
   generate agreements from Salesforce records.

## Resolution 3: Reset the Existing User's Password

Cause: If a user has an invalid Docusign password,
Gen actions will fail.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. [Log in to the connected Docusign account](https://account.docusign.com/#/username) as an
   administrator.
2. Go to Admin >  Users.
3. Select Actions next to the user's name and choose
   Reset Password.

   ![A screenshot displays the reset password option selected on the actions menu.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/izj1586134369853/page/images/genresetpassword.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODIxNTg0MTYsInNoZWFmIjoiaXpqMTU4NjEzNDM2OTg1MyJ9.XusU4q-iONegYhIjbSYb4FaiQrEDzr3gYq5f5pO79dQ&_LANG=enus "A screenshot displays the reset password option selected on the actions menu.")

   A message displays: A reset password email has been sent to (user
   name).

   You have completed the process. Docusign sends the user
   [a new activation email](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=ymi1586134213877.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to activate your Docusign CLM, Gen, Negotiate, or eSignature for Salesforce user.").

## Resolution 4: Restore System Administrator Status to Your Integration User

Cause: The Salesforce administrator user that you use to connect Docusign and Salesforce is called the integration user. Follow this link
for more information: [Apps Launcher Integration User Best Practices](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=rhi1657226496755.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how best to manage the integration user that connects your Salesforce and Docusign accounts.")

If you revoke the administrator status of your integration user, the integration fails
and Gen does not work.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. As an administrator, select the gear ![](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/izj1586134369853/page/images/salesforce-gear_.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODIxNTg0MTYsInNoZWFmIjoiaXpqMTU4NjEzNDM2OTg1MyJ9.XusU4q-iONegYhIjbSYb4FaiQrEDzr3gYq5f5pO79dQ&_LANG=enus) and choose
   Setup.
2. Restore System Administrator status to the installing Salesforce user. Or use
   different Salesforce administrator credentials to [reconnect the accounts](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=zfp1586134208988.html&_LANG=enus "Learn to connect your Salesforce organization to your Docusign account.").

   You have completed the process. You have restored the access of the
   integration user.

## Resolution 6: Check Your Salesforce Storage

Cause: Gen fails if you don't have enough storage available in your
Salesforce organization. When users generate agreements, document creation fails at the
Preview step. One of the following error messages display:

- There were some problems with your document generation. Unable to generate
  the following files: <file name>
- Unable to create a new ContentVersion for the file name : <file name>
  and SfContentDocumentId.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. As an administrator, select the gear ![](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/izj1586134369853/page/images/salesforce-gear_.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODIxNTg0MTYsInNoZWFmIjoiaXpqMTU4NjEzNDM2OTg1MyJ9.XusU4q-iONegYhIjbSYb4FaiQrEDzr3gYq5f5pO79dQ&_LANG=enus) and choose
   Setup.
2. Enter and select Storage Usage in the Quick Find field.
3. Check the Storage Type section.

   Note: If the Percent Used column adds up to
   100%, you are out of storage.

   You have completed the process. You have checked the storage capacity of
   your Salesforce organization. If there is not enough storage, delete data or contact
   your Salesforce representative to purchase additional storage.

## Resolution 7: Paste Your Template Content Into a New Word Document

Cause: Gen templates sometimes have hidden formatting in their Word
documents, like macros or hidden XML data. These hidden elements can prevent the template
from generating agreements. Pasting all text from your Word document into a new,
unformatted Word document fixes issues with hidden formatting.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. [Edit the Gen template Upload Documents page as a Salesforce
   administrator](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?topicId=nyj1621662912687.html&_LANG=enus).
2. Select a document name.

   The document preview page opens in a new tab or window.
3. Select Download and open the document in the Microsoft Word
   program.
4. Paste the template's text into a blank Word document:
   1. Copy all document content from your Gen template's Word document.
   2. Create a new blank Word document and paste the copied content.
   3. Save the new Word document to your device. Use the same file name as the
      original Gen template document.
5. Return to the Gen Upload Documents page and select
   X next to the old document version.![A screenshot of the Upload Your Word Templates section displays the Generated File Name, File Suffix, and File Format fields.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/izj1586134369853/page/images/deletewordtemplate.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODIxNTg0MTYsInNoZWFmIjoiaXpqMTU4NjEzNDM2OTg1MyJ9.XusU4q-iONegYhIjbSYb4FaiQrEDzr3gYq5f5pO79dQ&_LANG=enus "A screenshot of the Upload Your Word Templates section displays the Generated File Name, File Suffix, and File Format fields.")

   A message displays: File was successfully
   deleted.
6. Select Upload Files and follow the prompts to upload the new
   version from your device.

   A message displays: File was successfully uploaded.
7. Select Save and Close.
8. Select Finish and choose Save in the
   Do you want to save changes? dialog box.

   You have completed the process. Your old Gen template has been replaced with
   the new template, which has all the same content, but no hidden formatting.

## Resolution 8: Save Your Word Document in the Correct Format

Cause: You can only use .PDF or .DOCX file types in Gen templates.
If a Gen template document is saved as .DOC or another unsupported format, the template
fails.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. [Edit the Gen template Upload Documents page as a Salesforce
   administrator](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?topicId=nyj1621662912687.html&_LANG=enus).
2. Select a document name.

   The document preview page opens in a new tab or window.
3. Select Download and open the document in the Microsoft Word
   program.
4. Open your device's Downloads folder and verify that the file extension is
   .DOCX.
5. If the file is in a different format, open it and save a copy in .DOCX
   format.
6. Return to the Gen Upload Documents page and select
   X next to the old document version.
   ![A screenshot of the Upload Your Word Templates section displays the Generated File Name, File Suffix, and File Format fields.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/izj1586134369853/page/images/deletewordtemplate.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODIxNTg0MTYsInNoZWFmIjoiaXpqMTU4NjEzNDM2OTg1MyJ9.XusU4q-iONegYhIjbSYb4FaiQrEDzr3gYq5f5pO79dQ&_LANG=enus "A screenshot of the Upload Your Word Templates section displays the Generated File Name, File Suffix, and File Format fields.")

   A message displays: File was successfully
   deleted.
7. Select Upload Files and follow the prompts to upload the new
   version from your device.

   A message displays: File was successfully uploaded.
8. Select Save and Close.
9. Select Finish and choose Save in the
   Do you want to save changes? dialog box.

   You have completed the process. You have replaced your old Gen template with
   a .DOCX version.

   The document preview page opens in a new tab or window.
3. Select Download, open the document in Microsoft Word, and
   select the Review tab.
4. Select Accept and choose Accept All

## Resolution 10: Test Configuration Using a Simple Template

Cause: You may have a problem with your Gen template or a template
Word document.

Create a simple Word document with only a few merge tags to test your Gen integration. If
your simple template works, you know that your Gen integration is working. If the simple
template fails, you must examine your integration. If the simple template works, check
your failing Gen template for issues.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. [Open Docusign Apps Launcher as
   an administrator.](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=etv1586134206174.html&_LANG=enus "Learn to open the Docusign Apps Launcher for Salesforce package in your organization.")
2. Select Docusign Gen Templates.
3. Follow these instructions to [create a new Gen
   template](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=lig1586134209279.html&_LANG=enus "Learn to build a Gen template using a Microsoft Word document.").

   Note: When creating the new
   template, only add two or three basic Anchor Text tags to your Word document.
4. Test the new template by generating an agreement.

   You have completed the process. If the new, simple Gen template successfully
   generates an agreement, troubleshoot the original Word document. Check for hidden
   formatting, Macros, unsaved tracked changes or other problems. If the simple Gen
   template also fails, check for issues with the Gen integration. See if the template
   contains deleted Salesforce fields.

## Resolution 11: Disconnect Docusign Administrator Credentials from Other Accounts

Cause: Gen fails if you use one set of Docusign
administrator credentials to connect multiple Salesforce organizations. For example, if
you use one set of Docusign credentials to connect to a Salesforce
sandbox and a Salesforce production organization. The connection conflict causes problems
with document generation. Disconnect from one organization and re-connect with different
Docusign administrator credentials.

Example: You use Docusign administrator
credentials to connect to Salesforce Org A. You use the same credentials to connect with
Salesforce Org B.

Result: The most-recently-connected organization operates normally.
All other integrations fail. The Salesforce Org A integration fails and the Org B
integration operates normally.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. Log in to the Salesforce organization requiring a new integration user.
2. [Use the Disconnect Link to Disconnect and Reconnect Docusign and Salesforce](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=ntr1586134210309.html&_LANG=enus "Use this procedure to disconnect and reconnect your Docusign account from your Salesforce organization.")

   You have completed the process. You have reconnected your Docusign account to the desired org.

## Resolution 12: Use Production Administrator Credentials for Your Integration User

Cause: Gen fails if you use Docusign Demo
administrator credentials for your integration user. An error displays in the Document
Preview step of the template creation process: There were some problems with
your document generation.

Use this procedure to fix the issue:

1. [Open Docusign Apps Launcher as
   an administrator](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=etv1586134206174.html&_LANG=enus "Learn to open the Docusign Apps Launcher for Salesforce package in your organization.").
2. [Disconnect Your Docusign Account and Preserve User Settings with the Apps Launcher TroubleShooting Tool](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=oks1610512362945.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to disconnect Docusign from Salesforce while preserving user settings with the Docusign TroubleShooting tool.").
3. Log out of Salesforce.
4. Clear your browser cache and log back in to Salesforce.
5. Follow the instructions to [Connect Salesforce
   and Docusign](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=izj1586134369853&topicId=zfp1586134208988.html&_LANG=enus "Learn to connect your Salesforce organization to your Docusign account."), using Docusign production
   administrator credentials.

   You have completed the process. You have reconnected your Docusign account to prevent Gen template creation errors in the Preview step.
