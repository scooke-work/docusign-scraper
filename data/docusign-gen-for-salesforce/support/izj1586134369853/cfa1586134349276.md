# Learn About Adding Child Objects to a Gen Template

Learn about adding up to four levels of child objects and two levels of related lists.

You can add child objects to your Gen template. Child objects are objects related to a parent
object. For example, opportunities are child objects of accounts.

You can add child objects with up to four levels of relation to the parent, and two levels of
related lists. The following are examples of possible combinations:

Table 1. Child Relation Combinations

| Object Relation | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Root | Opportunity |
| 1 : 1 | Opportunity >  Account |
| 1 : 1 : 1 | Opportunity >  Account >  Parent Account |
| 1 : 1 : 1 : 1 | Opportunity  >  Account >  Parent Account >  Owner |
| 1 : Many | Opportunity >  Opportunity Contact Roles |
| 1 : Many : 1 | Opportunity >  Opportunity Contact Roles >  Contact |
| 1 : Many : 1 : 1 | Opportunity >  Opportunity Contact Roles >  Contact >  Reports To |
| 1 : Many : Many | Account  >  Contacts >  Cases Note: This example starts from the Account object. |
| 1 : Many : Many : 1 | Account  >  Contacts >  Cases >  Owner Note: This example starts from the Account object. |
