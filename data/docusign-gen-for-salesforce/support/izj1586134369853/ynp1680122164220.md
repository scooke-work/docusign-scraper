# Use Sync Domains to Rebuild Your CLM or CLM Essentials Integration After a Salesforce Domain Name Change

Learn to fix domain integration issues using the Sync Domains feature. Sync Domains
fixes issues with Enhanced Domains and any changes that you make to your Salesforce
domain.

## Before you begin

[Install Docusign Apps Launcher for Salesforce
v5.2](https://login.salesforce.com/packaging/installPackage.apexp?p0=04t3u000002wLcp)

You can change your Salesforce domain name to update your brand, adopt [Enhanced Domains](https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=sf.domain_name_enhanced.htm&type=5), or enable partitioned domains. When
your My Domain name changes, your Salesforce-hosted URLs also change. You must
rebuild the connection to your Docusign account.

The Sync Domains feature of the Docusign
TroubleShooting tool rebuilds your connection to Docusign after a
domain change.

Use this procedure to rebuild your CLM or CLM
Essentials integration with the Sync Domains
feature:

1. Access the My Domain page as a Salesforce
   administrator:
   1.       Setup.
   2. Enter and select My Domain in the Quick Find
      field.
2. Enable redirects for your domain:

   Note: The Domain Name System (DNS) server takes up to 72 hours to update
   the connection between Docusign and Salesforce. Keep the
   redirect setting enabled during this time.

   1. Routing: Select Edit.
   2. Select the Redirect previous My Domain URLs to your current
      My Domain check box.
   3. Select Save. The My Domain page opens.
3. Access the Visualforce Pages page as a Salesforce
   administrator:
   1.    The TroubleShooting page opens.
6. Sync Domains: Select Execute.

   The page refreshes. A message displays: Successfully synced
   domains on Docusign account.
7. (Optional): After 72 hours, uncheck the Redirect previous My Domain
   URLs to your current My Domain box.

You have completed the process. You have rebuilt the CLM or CLM
Essentials integration with your Salesforce organization.
