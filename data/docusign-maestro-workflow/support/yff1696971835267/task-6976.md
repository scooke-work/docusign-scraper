# Set Branding for a Workflow

Choose a signing brand for your workflow to make sure all related emails and signing sessions use your custom logos and colors.

Workflow Builder uses the signing brands configured on your account to control the visual appearance of workflow emails and signing sessions. This branding includes logos, header color, and button color. You set branding at the workflow level from the Workflow Builder designer, rather than configuring it separately on each step.

You can choose to replace the branding on your templates with your workflow branding. However, if the eSignature template is configured to restrict branding changes, the template's original brand will always take precedence. If your chosen brand fails to load for some reason, Workflow Builder will automatically use your account's default look. If that isn't available either, it will fall back to standard Docusign branding.

## Before you begin

- Your admin has created one or more signing brands for your account (Admin > Brands > Signing).
- You have permission to edit and publish the workflow.

1. Open your workflow in Workflow Builder.
2. At the top of the screen, select More (...) > Branding.
3. Select the signing brand that you want to use.

   The preview updates to show the signing and email logos and the header and button colors for the selected brand.
4. (Optional) check the box for Override custom template branding if you want this brand to replace the branding defined on templates used in this workflow.
5. Review the preview to confirm that the logos and colors look correct.
6. Select Apply.
7. Publish or republish the workflow so the branding changes take effect.

The workflow uses the selected signing brand for its emails and signing sessions, subject to any template-level brand locks and fallback behavior.

## Next steps

Important:

If an eSignature template owner or administrator has disabled Allow senders to edit the logo and brand colors in the template’s Advanced Options, that template’s branding is locked and cannot be changed by the workflow branding. This restriction persists even if you select Override custom template branding.
