# Create a Basic Workflow

Learn the basics of how to create an agreement workflow. Process creators and account administrators can create workflows to automate the agreement process.

Workflow Builder connects Docusign applications and third-party extension apps to automate your agreement processes and boost productivity. This topic covers the basics of creating a workflow for process creators and account administrators. More detailed information on specific workflow steps are presented in subsequent topics.

Before you begin
:   - Creating workflows requires [specific user permissions](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=gbb1696973048215.html&_LANG=enus "Read about the different roles involved with creating and using workflows with Docusign Workflow Builder. Each role requires specific user permissions."). Account administrators by default have all required permissions. For account users, if you cannot follow the instructions in this topic, check with your account administrator to review and adjust your user permissions. Review the topic [Workflow Builder Roles and Permission Requirements](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=gbb1696973048215.html&_LANG=enus "Read about the different roles involved with creating and using workflows with Docusign Workflow Builder. Each role requires specific user permissions.").
    - How will your workflow be initiated? Manually by someone on your account? Or through an external system that will pass data into the process? Reference [Workflow Start Methods](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=ztb1727892686033.html&_LANG=enus "Review the different automated and manual trigger methods available to initiate processes in Workflow Builder, including links, events, API calls, and CLM integrations.").
    - Consider the roles that will participate in your agreement process. Who are the individuals and entities that will provide information, sign documents, and participate in any other way? In a workflow, these are called "participants". Review the [Define Workflow Participants](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=ypt1698264964168.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to define, configure, and manage participant roles within your workflow steps to control how users interact with your agreement automation.").
    - Workflows are built using preexisting web forms, eSignature templates, and document templates in your account. Before you create a workflow, you will need access to the web forms and templates you want to use in your agreement process.
    - For workflows that will be triggered through an external system that will pass data into the process, what data will be required at runtime? These are the starting variables you define for your workflow. Reference [General Limitations and Considerations for Workflow Builder](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=otm1696972807664.html&_LANG=enus "Review important account limits, supported regions, and functional considerations before building automated processes in Workflow Builder."), "Data types and character limits for variables".
    - Your organization's account plan determines how many published and paused workflows you can have at a time. Once your account reaches this limit, you must unpublish a workflow before you can publish a new one. Reference [Unpublish or Delete a Workflow](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=kbn1698257312051.html&_LANG=enus "As a process builder, you can unpublish any of your paused workflows. You can edit and republish unpublished workflows, or you can permanently delete them.").

1. Log in to your Docusign account.
2. Select the Agreements tab and then select  Workflows in the left-hand navigation.
3. In the Workflows list, select Create Workflow.
4. Select + Blank Workflow > Continue.

   Your draft workflow displays on the canvas with the Add workflow start selection.

   Note: When creating a new workflow, you also have options to use one of our [pre-built workflow templates](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=irb1736981148403.html&_LANG=enus "Discover how pre-built templates in Workflow Builder provide ready-made blueprints to rapidly automate your business and agreement processes."), [create a workflow from one of your eSignature templates](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=tsk3aff5ded-8b1a-47e2-89a4-a6bca6c990be.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to generate a fully configured workflow directly from an existing eSignature template to quickly automate your signing process."), or [import a workflow](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=zoi1742412999176.html&_LANG=enus "Learn how to import a workflow into your account.").
5. Select Add workflow start, select the workflow start method that supports your business process.
   1. For API and CLM workflow starts, add variables for the data inputs coming from an external system.
   2. Select Apply.
6. Select the Manage workflow participants icon to add the participant roles for your process.

   Most steps require you to specify one or more participants to be used in the step. You can use any participant already configured, or you can add new ones through the step configuration. ![Screenshot of a draft workflow and indicating the Manage workflow participants icon.](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/yff1696971835267/page/images/workflowsetupicon.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE4MDU2ODAsInNoZWFmIjoieWZmMTY5Njk3MTgzNTI2NyJ9.SWkv8ly-9Q6HIirAz76NRfllSymWjesNQRmg4dYA8MA&_LANG=enus "Screenshot of a draft workflow and indicating the Manage workflow participants icon.")
7. (Optional) To control how emails and signing sessions look, select More (...) > Branding and choose a signing brand before publishing.

   See the article [Set Branding for a Workflow](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=yff1696971835267&topicId=task-6976.html&_LANG=enus "Choose a signing brand for your workflow to make sure all related emails and signing sessions use your custom logos and colors.") for details.
8. Select Add a step and then select the step type you want to add to your workflow.

   - To add a step between existing blocks, hover over the arrow on the canvas and click the +  icon.
   - To delete a step, select the step's three-dot action menu and choose Delete.![](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/yff1696971835267/page/images/add_new_step.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE4MDU2ODAsInNoZWFmIjoieWZmMTY5Njk3MTgzNTI2NyJ9.SWkv8ly-9Q6HIirAz76NRfllSymWjesNQRmg4dYA8MA&_LANG=enus)

   - The available step types depend on the applications and extensions authorized for your account.
   - The Add New Step panel includes a Home tab with all steps, and additional tabs that present steps in clear categories.
   - The Suggested next step section recommends the most relevant next actions based on your previous steps.
9. For each step you add, select Configure to configure the step.

   The configuration is different for each step type. Refer to the detailed step configuration topics for more information.
10. (Optional) Add a Path End step to all end points of your workflow.

    The Path End step is a visual indicator of final steps in a workflow. If you do not add these markers, they are added automatically when you publish a workflow. This utility step has no effect on workflow function; its purpose is to help you visually structure your design on the canvas. ![](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/yff1696971835267/page/images/path_end_example.png?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE4MDU2ODAsInNoZWFmIjoieWZmMTY5Njk3MTgzNTI2NyJ9.SWkv8ly-9Q6HIirAz76NRfllSymWjesNQRmg4dYA8MA&_LANG=enus)
11. Once you've added and configured all your steps, your workflow will look something like this example, which uses all three Docusign apps (Web Forms, ID Verification, eSignature) and the Google file archive partner app:

    ![](https://docusign-be-prod.zoominsoftware.io/api/bundle/yff1696971835267/page/images/sampleworkflow.gif?token=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJjdXN0b21lciI6ImRvY3VzaWduX3Byb2R1Y3Rpb24iLCJleHAiOjE3ODE4MDU2ODAsInNoZWFmIjoieWZmMTY5Njk3MTgzNTI2NyJ9.SWkv8ly-9Q6HIirAz76NRfllSymWjesNQRmg4dYA8MA&_LANG=enus)
12. Select the three-dot action menu at the top of the screen and select Save and Exit. Enter a name for your workflow draft and select Save & Exit.

    Note: You can edit your workflow name when you review and publish the workflow.

Your workflow is saved and appears in the Workflows list as an unpublished draft.
