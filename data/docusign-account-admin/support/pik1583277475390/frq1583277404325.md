# Document Retention

Read about the document retention policy and how account administrators can change it to
fit their business needs.

With a document retention policy, you can set the number of days to retain completed,
declined, and voided documents. Setting a document retention period is optional. By default,
Docusign securely retains all customer documents on the service for as
long as the account is active.

When an account is closed, Docusign may retain all sent, signed, and voided envelopes for a period of approximately two years from the date of account closure to meet legal, compliance, auditing, or security obligations. This retention period may be further extended if legally required.

If you choose to set a retention period, any affected envelopes are placed in a [purge queue](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?bundleId=oeq1643226594604&topicId=rlk1578456343945.html&_LANG=enus) prior to actual purging. After expiration of the purge queue period, the Docusign system deletes the
documents in the envelopes.

The maximum retention period that you can set is 9,999 days.

Note: Users can read [Purge Envelopes](https://support.docusign.com/s/document-item?topicId=edo1578456348066.html&bundleId=oeq1643226594604&_LANG=enus) for information on purging.

Note: For more information, read the [Best Practices for Docusign Document Retention and Purging](https://dsucustomers.docusign.com/km-guide-best-practices-for-docusign-document-retention-and-purging)
whitepaper.
